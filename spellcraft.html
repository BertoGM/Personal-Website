Spellcraft Arena

Unity · C# · Netcode for GameObjects · Mirror Networking

Early Development
About the Game

Spellcraft Arena is a multiplayer fantasy battle royale where magic is your only weapon. Players drop into a shrinking arena, scavenge randomly spawned spells and abilities, and fight until one player remains — all in a fast-paced, chaotic environment where the best spell combination wins.

The game is inspired by spell-based survival and battle royale mechanics — think Noita meets Spellbreak — with an emphasis on emergent gameplay through dynamic spell combinations, strategic positioning, and quick decision-making under pressure.

Core Gameplay
  • ▸ Battle royale format — last mage standing wins
  • ▸ Shrinking map boundary forces players into conflict
  • ▸ Randomly spawned spells and abilities across the map
  • ▸ Dynamic spell combinations — chain effects for powerful synergies
  • ▸ Loot progression — upgrade spells mid-match for increasing power
  • ▸ Stylized fantasy world with fast, readable combat visuals
Key Systems
Spell Combination System

Spells interact with each other — fire ignites oil slicks, lightning chains between wet enemies, wind redirects projectiles. Emergent combinations are the game's strategic heart.

Multiplayer Networking

Real-time multiplayer via Unity's Netcode for GameObjects. Players, projectiles, and spell effects are all synchronized across clients with lag compensation and server-authoritative logic.

Shrinking Zone

A classic battle royale safe zone that contracts over time — implemented as an animated boundary that deals escalating damage to players outside it, forcing increasingly intense encounters.

Randomized Loot

Spell drops, ability upgrades, and items spawn dynamically around the map on a weighted rarity table — so no two matches play out the same way.

Tech Stack
Unity C# Netcode for GameObjects Unity Physics ScriptableObjects Unity VFX Graph

ScriptableObjects are used extensively for spell definitions — each spell's behavior, visuals, and combination rules are data-driven, making it easy to add new spells without touching game logic.

Current Status

Currently in early development — core movement, basic spell casting, and a prototype shrinking zone are implemented in a local build. Multiplayer networking is the current focus, getting reliable synchronization working before expanding the spell system and map design. A playable demo is the short-term milestone.
